compass

A compass is a navigation tool that uses a magnetized needle to align with the Earth's magnetic field, pointing to magnetic north and helping users determine cardinal directions (North, South, East, West) and navigate geographic locations

It consists of a compass rose showing directions and often degree markings for more precise orientation.

Though anciently used in China for divination, its primary modern use is for navigation, especially when GPS or other technology isn't available.

A compass relies on the Earth's magnetic field. The Earth acts like a giant magnet with a north and south magnetic pole.

Inside the compass is a magnetized needle or element.

This needle is free to rotate and will naturally align itself with the Earth's magnetic field, with one end always pointing toward the magnetic north pole.

The compass shows these directions on a compass rose, which has markings for the cardinal directions (N, S, E, W) and often ordinal directions (NE, SE, SW, NW). 

Key Components
                Magnetized Needle:
                The primary component that indicates direction.

Compass Rose :A diagram showing the cardinal and ordinal directions, often with letters like N, S, E, and W.

Displays degree markings, with North typically at 0 degrees and increasing clockwise (East = 90°, South = 180°, West = 270°).

A protective housing, often made of plastic or metal, that holds the components together.

Navigation: Helps people find their way and navigate through different terrains, such as on water, mountains, or on foot.

Essential for orienting a map and following routes accurately.

Used in surveys to determine direction and orientation.
